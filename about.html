About Me

I am a Denver based UX/UI designer who is passionate about designing for change. Ask those closest to me and they will tell you that I care deeply about a lot of different issues including, but not limited to environmental justice, social justice, and women’s issues. They will also tell you that I am genuinely self-reflective and always striving to improve myself and my work. I love food, friends, nature, and playing the ukelele. I also love taking pictures of nature and I took all the photos on this page!

My Roots

I am a former science educator that found design completely by accident and then realized it was what I had been working towards all along. Before I get into that part of the story though, let me tell you a bit more about my roots. I grew up the daughter of a marine biologist and community nurse. Both incredibly passionate about the way in which they were contributing to the world around them. The combination of traveling the world to go out into the field with my dad and the non-stop playlist of NPR shows gave me the privilege of learning about the world and all the different kinds of people that live in it.

From Teaching to Designing

I decided to become a teacher and stayed on this track all throughout my education at the College of Education at the University of Maryland. As a graduate student, I narrowed my focus on climate change science curriculum and instruction. I was lucky enough to work on designing curriculum on this very topic with some of the most progressive minds in the field. Teaching encompassed some of my greatest strengths: building connections and relationships through empathy and listening, crafting research driven curriculum which I constantly iterated upon through testing in the field (my classroom), and being creative and thinking on my feet when something didn’t go as planned (which was pretty much everyday). Though my experience in education was invaluable and fulfilling, there were aspects that caused me to question the sustainability of it for me as a career. I felt an itch to explore what else I could be a part of.

As I alluded to before, I was introduced to the field of UX/ UI completely by accident and during a time when I was really unsure of where my future was going. In searching for new inspiration, I stumbled upon the UX/UI community in my city and, to my delight, found other former educators who had made the transition. From there, everything started falling into place.

Designing All Along

As I dove into the world of UX/UI design, I started to realize just how many times I had wanted to be a designer before. For example, a design problem I once experienced happened when I was writing the curriculum for my sixth grade class and was struggling to find resources accessible for young students. I started to dream of being able to design my own resources from websites that tracked climatic shifts to websites that connected kids across the world to share their stories of how climate change was impacting their communities. I didn’t realize at the time that I was already starting my passion for UX/ UI design through my desire to create platforms that could inform teachers, young students, and even everyday citizens about these issues. I started to create a hybrid curriculum using what was helpful from various outside sources as well as my own curriculums that I had created in the past. I didn’t realize it at the time but I was already conducting user research and even usability tests with, not only my students, but also other teachers that I was sharing my work with and asking them about how I could improve it.

Social Impact & Design

It seems to me that design can be a powerful force in informing people and improving people’s lives as well as giving them new opportunities: this is the kind of work I would like to be a part. I believe designers have a responsibility to think about the voiceless, marginalized, and underrepresented people in the world and make sure they get a voice through design. This is the work I have done as an educator and environmentalist. I would like to use design to influence the future by changing the narrative of who is represented, how they are represented, and whether or not resources are accessible to them; or in other words, I would like to be a socially responsible designer.
